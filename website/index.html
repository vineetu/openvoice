How it works

Three seconds from thought to text.

1

Press your hotkey

A global shortcut starts recording from any app. Push-to-talk is available too.

2

Speak naturally

No length limits. A floating indicator shows you're recording, then transcribing.

3

Text appears at your cursor

The transcript is pasted directly into whatever app you're using. That's it.

Capabilities

Dictation and transcription happen on your Mac.
ⓘCleanup, Rewrite, and Ask Jot default to Apple Intelligence on-device. If you opt into a cloud provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, or Ollama) in Settings, the text processed by that feature is sent to that provider using your API key. Your choice, your key, your provider.

Audio never leaves the device. Jot runs Parakeet on the Apple Neural Engine for fast, private transcription.

 🔒

On-device privacy

Audio is transcribed locally using the Neural Engine. Nothing crosses the network. Run Little Snitch — you'll see silence.

 ⌨

Works everywhere

The global hotkey works in any application. Toggle recording or hold to push-to-talk. Cancel anytime with Escape.

 ✏

Rewrite

Select text, speak an instruction (or use the fixed prompt), and an LLM rewrites it in place via a global shortcut. Voice instruction or fixed prompt. Prompts are editable for power users.

 ✨

Transcript cleanup

Optional LLM pass strips "um"s and false starts, fixes grammar, and keeps your tone intact. Runs locally with Ollama if you prefer. Customize the prompt or reset to the shipped default.

 📚

Home with recordings

Banner, hotkey glance, and your full searchable recordings history — all on Home. Replay audio, rename entries, or re-transcribe with a different model.

 ✨

Ask Jot

Ask anything about Jot in plain English. On-device by default; cloud providers optional.

 ☁

Cloud providers

OpenAI, Anthropic, Gemini, or Ollama — your key, your provider, always optional.

 🧭

One window, built-in help

Home, Library, Settings, and a prose Help tab live in a single sidebar window. Every Settings field carries an info popover with a "Learn more →" deep-link into Help.

Free forever

Your Mac already has the hardware to transcribe speech. Jot simply puts it to use. No subscription, no usage limits, no catch. Open source under the MIT license.
